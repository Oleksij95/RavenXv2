Check Engine Light is on (Inspection)

How it works

When this light is on, car's monitoring system will have 'fault codes' that indicate the nature of the problem. Your car may have one or more problems and hence there can be multiple codes.
Your mechanic will:

  • Read the codes using scanner
  • Interpret the code
  • Provide a preliminary diagnosis and repair estime

If you approve the repair estimate, they will:

  • Replace the faulty part(s)
  • Clear the memory of any fault codes
  • Turn the check engine light off
  • Let you drive the car for a few days to see if the light comes back on or not

Please note:
It may take several days of driving the car for the monitoring system to verify that the repair has fixed all the problems. If the monitoring system fails any of its internal tests, it will turn the light back on. This means that the car has more problems that were not evident during the first evaluation. Your mechanic will have to perform further diagnosis and create new repair estimates for resolving the problems found. In some rare cases, multiple visits may be required to diagnose and resolve the stubborn check engine light issues.

Frequently asked questions

What does Check Engine Light is on entail

The mechanic will be able to remotely connect to your car (if you have our scanner and paid license to use) to determine the source and cause of lit check engine light and then test other system components as identifying specific symptoms and problems. After inspection, the mechanic will provide a detailed report that describes the diagnosis, along with the volume and cost of the necessary repairs.

Keep in mind

The Check Engine Light can illuminate for minor issues. The most common and easiest to resolve is a loose gas cap. Almost every vehicle that does not have a dedicated light for a loose gas cap will light up the Check Engine Light when the problem occurs. Simply tighten the cap until it clicks two or three times to take care of the issue. After you tighten the cap, the car should be switched off and turned back on two or three times. If the Check Engine Light still has not gone out, there is a problem that must be diagnosed by a professional mechanic.

How it's done

When the Check Engine Light is on, your car’s monitoring system will have "fault codes" that indicate the nature of the problem. Your car may have one or more problems and hence there can be multiple codes. The mechanic will read the codes using a scanner, interpret the codes, and provide a diagnosis. If you approve the repair estimate, they will replace the faulty part(s), clear the memory of any fault codes, turn the Check Engine Light off, and let you drive the car for a few days to see if the light comes back on or not. Please note: It may take several days of driving the car for the monitoring system to verify that the repair has fixed all the problems. If the monitoring system fails any of its internal tests, it will turn the light back on. This means that the car has more problems that were not evident during the first evaluation. The mechanic will have to perform further diagnosis and create new repair estimates for resolving the problems found. In some rare cases, multiple visits may be required to diagnose and resolve the stubborn Check Engine Light issues.
